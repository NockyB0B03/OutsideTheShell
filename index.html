The Challenge

Why do travellers stay inside their shell?

Modern tourism often keeps people at arm's length from the very cultures they've come to experience. Guided bus tours, tourist traps, and language barriers leave millions of travellers with nothing but a souvenir and a vague memory. We asked ourselves: why is genuine connection so rare?

01

No chance to meet locals

Traditional travel infrastructure isolates tourists from the communities they visit, offering no real bridge to authentic local life.

02

Shyness & social barriers

Even when the opportunity exists, fear of awkward interactions keeps travellers within their comfort zone — and inside their shell.

03

Cultural blind spots

Without context, travellers miss the richness of what surrounds them. Surface-level sightseeing leaves cultures unexplored.

The Shell Pendant

A symbol that connects us all

Every participant — tourist and local alike — receives a pendant shaped like a shell: the ancient pilgrim's sign of journey and openness. It's more than a badge. It's a technology and a philosophy.

When two or more participants meet and walk together, the pendants establish a connection, creating a shared space for the duration of the encounter.

Recognise fellow participants at a glance
Shared music jams & real-time photo exchange
Route synchronisation between companions
GPS tracker with emergency distress signal to authorities
